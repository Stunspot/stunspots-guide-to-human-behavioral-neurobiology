## **3. Chemical Signal Taxonomy**

To accurately model chemical modulation within an active inference framework, signaling molecules must be classified by their spatial range, temporal dynamics, transmission modes, and target receptor properties. This taxonomy replaces single-molecule reductionism with a systems-level ontology where a molecule's name is never an explanation, but rather the entry point for a mechanism question.

Classical neurotransmitters like glutamate and gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) operate primarily through point-to-point wiring transmission across distinct synaptic clefts. They alter postsynaptic membrane potentials within milliseconds via ionotropic receptors to carry fast sensory or motor information. Conversely, neuromodulators such as dopamine, norepinephrine, acetylcholine, and serotonin operate extensively through volume transmission.16 Released from axonal varicosities that often lack traditional postsynaptic specializations, they diffuse through the extracellular fluid to act on widespread grids of metabotropic G-protein coupled receptors (GPCRs) over timelines of seconds to minutes.2 This dual architecture allows neuromodulators to act as localized or global gain controllers, adjusting the signal-to-noise ratio and structural sensitivity of classical synaptic networks without carrying the raw sensory or motor signals themselves.16

Neuropeptides, such as endogenous opioids, oxytocin, vasopressin, and orexins, represent a highly specialized class of large-molecule signaling elements synthesized in the soma and packaged into large dense-core vesicles (LDCVs).20 Neuropeptides require high-frequency, sustained neuronal firing for exocytosis, are released extrasynaptically, and undergo no rapid reuptake mechanisms, relying instead on slow enzymatic degradation. Acting exclusively through high-affinity GPCRs, they exhibit prolonged half-lives and can diffuse over long distances within the extracellular matrix, functioning as structural state-switchers that signal high-priority ecological conditions such as safety, metabolic crises, or critical social bonds.

Endocrine and metabolic hormones—including cortisol, insulin, leptin, ghrelin, and glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1)—are synthesized in peripheral tissues or specialized hypothalamic nuclei, entering systemic circulation to coordinate whole-organism responses.8 They cross the blood-brain barrier (BBB) via specialized transporter mechanisms or enter through circumventricular organs, acting directly on metabolic hubs such as the arcuate nucleus of the hypothalamus and the ventral tegmental area (VTA).12 Operating over hours, days, or weeks, these signals provide continuous feedback regarding systemic energy reserves, gut nutrient passage, and overall allostatic demand.8 They fundamentally shift the organism's behavioral economics by altering the baseline affordability and expected cost structures of all motor and cognitive policies.12

Immune cytokines, including interleukin-1 beta (IL-1beta), interleukin-6 (IL-6), and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha), serve as real-time molecular sensors of peripheral tissue damage, pathogen invasion, and systemic inflammation.14 Released by peripheral immune cells, they signal the central nervous system via fast vagal afferent pathways or humorally by altering blood-brain barrier permeability via brain endothelial cell activation.22 Once registered centrally, cytokines trigger microglial activation and neuroinflammatory cascades that directly reprogram midbrain monoaminergic centers.14 This immune-motivational retuning suppresses high-energy external foraging or social policies, shifting the regulatory engine toward metabolic conservation and localized tissue repair.15

## **4. Dopamine: Policy Precision, Vigor, Learning, and Effort Valuation**

The midbrain dopaminergic system, originating within the substantia nigra pars compacta (SNc) and the ventral tegmental area (VTA), serves as a primary computational element for estimating policy value and regulating behavioral drive.18 Popular narratives characterizing dopamine as a reward-generating "pleasure juice," or advocating for behavioral optimization via "dopamine detoxes," are neurobiologically invalid. Dopamine does not encode sensory pleasure or hedonic consummation.28 Instead, it serves as a multi-pathway parameter that determines whether a selected behavioral or cognitive policy is worth energizing, learning, repeating, updating, or abandoning.5

At the phasic timescale (milliseconds to seconds), dopaminergic neurons encode a formal reward prediction error (delta_n), operationalized in temporal difference reinforcement learning and active inference frameworks as the discrepancy between realized environmental returns (r_n) and the prior expected value of a given state (v_n) 18:

delta_n = r_n - v_n

This phasic transient updates the synaptic weights (w_n) of corticostriatal loops following a standard delta learning rule modulated by the learning rate (eta) and the current state vector (x_n) 18:

w_(n+1) = w_n + eta * x_n * delta_n

Concurrently, tonic dopamine levels—the ambient, extrasynaptic concentration maintained by baseline pacemaker firing and dopamine transporter (DAT) clearance rates—encode the long-run average rate of reward (R) per unit of time within the local ecology.4 In free-operant behavioral environments, R acts mathematically as the opportunity cost of time.4 When tonic dopamine is high, the environment is estimated to be highly productive; consequently, every second spent in a state of behavioral inactivity or sloth incurs a steep opportunity cost.30 This computational signal biases the selection of response latencies, driving action invigoration and increasing overall motor and cognitive vigor, even when executing the action requires higher absolute bioenergetic expenditures.29

The computational effects of dopamine are strictly dependent on structural pathway routing and receptor distribution families.5 The nigrostriatal pathway projects from the SNc to the dorsal striatum, gating the execution of lower-level motor habits and transitioning policies from model-based deliberative control to model-free automated execution. The mesolimbic pathway maps VTA inputs to the nucleus accumbens (NAc), functioning as a key hub for calculating the Expected Value of Control (EVC) and determining the willingness to exert physical or cognitive effort for delayed rewards.5 The mesocortical pathway connects the VTA to the prefrontal cortex (PFC), where it coordinates working memory maintenance and the hierarchical organization of goal-directed actions.5

Within these projection targets, dopamine interacts with two distinct receptor families that exhibit highly divergent affinities and downstream intracellular signaling cascades 5:

* **D1-Like Receptor Family (D1 and D5):** Coupled to G_alpha s/olf proteins, these receptors stimulate adenylyl cyclase activity, increasing intracellular cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP). They exhibit a low affinity for endogenous dopamine, requiring high-concentration phasic bursts for activation. In the PFC, intermediate to high D1 activation creates a robust energy barrier around current working memory networks.6 This configuration protects active target representations against external distractors, stabilizing the current governing policy.6 In the striatum, D1 signaling activates the direct "Go" pathway, facilitating action execution.5  
* **D2-Like Receptor Family (D2, D3, and D4):** Coupled to G_alpha i/o proteins, these receptors inhibit adenylyl cyclase and decrease cAMP production. They display a high affinity for dopamine, remaining highly occupied under low baseline tonic concentrations. In the prefrontal cortex, a D2-dominated state lowers the local energy barrier, toggling the network into a flexible, labile configuration that permits working memory updating and policy switching.5 In the striatum, D2 stimulation downregulates the indirect "NoGo" pathway, removing behavioral inhibition.5 Presynaptic D2 autoreceptors provide a negative feedback loop, suppressing subsequent dopamine synthesis and exocytosis to limit signal duration.

Dopamine dynamics are tightly constrained by the metabolic ledger of Report D. The allocation of prefrontal working memory loops and deep cognitive planning episodes carries a heavy computational and metabolic control cost.5 The brain manages this expenditure by executing a formal cost-benefit optimization process: it weights the subjective value of a potential reward against the energetic cost required to obtain it.5 Dopamine tone within the striatum and medial prefrontal cortex shifts this valuation equation.5 High dopaminergic transmission increases the subjective valuation of the reward and downregulates the perceived weight of the physical or cognitive effort cost, rendering high-energy policies computationally affordable.5

Conversely, states of absolute bioenergetic depletion, localized prefrontal glycogen exhaustion, or peripheral metabolic signaling directly alter this dopaminergic cost-benefit engine.12 For example, elevated peripheral inflammatory cytokines or adipose-derived leptin act on midbrain nodes to reduce dopamine synthesis and increase surface expression of the dopamine transporter (DAT), accelerating clearance and flattening the tonic dopamine signal.13 This biochemical shift steepens the effort-discounting curve, making even low-cost tasks appear computationally unviable, thereby biasing the organism toward energy-preservation policies.15

## **5. Norepinephrine: Arousal, Urgency, Volatility, and Adaptive Gain**

The central noradrenergic system, organized primarily within the locus coeruleus (LC) in the brainstem, serves as the brain's principal real-time gain-control mechanism and arousal engine.1 It does not merely generate generic panic or stress states; rather, the LC-NE system acts as a highly specialized chemical tuning parameter that tracks environmental uncertainty, gauges state volatility, and reconfigures network topology to optimize active inference and behavioral survival policies.17

According to Adaptive Gain Theory, the locus coeruleus alternates between two distinct operational modes—tonic and phasic—to optimize behavioral exploitation and environmental exploration under changing conditions:

* **Phasic LC Mode:** Characterized by low baseline tonic firing punctuated by high-amplitude, temporally precise bursts of norepinephrine in response to task-relevant stimuli or salient prediction errors. This mode projects to cortical sites to function as a "network reset" signal, enhancing the signal-to-noise ratio of specific target populations, sharpening sensory attention, and reinforcing the execution of the current behavioral policy.  
* **Tonic LC Mode:** Characterized by elevated, sustained baseline firing rates without clear stimulus-locked bursts. This mode occurs when the environment undergoes rapid structural transitions or when existing predictive models suffer catastrophic failures (high unexpected uncertainty or environmental volatility).31 Elevated tonic norepinephrine diffuses globally via volume transmission, disrupting settled cortical configurations and lowering the gain on current goals.2 This network destabilization increases behavioral variability, shifting the organism away from exploiting low-yield local patches and toward active environmental exploration.29

Norepinephrine dynamically updates cortical gain through an array of adrenergic receptors (ARs) that exhibit unique binding affinities and regional distribution maps:

* **alpha2-Adrenergic Receptors (alpha2-AR):** High-affinity, G_i-coupled receptors that include both presynaptic autoreceptors and postsynaptic elements. Under conditions of optimal, moderate norepinephrine release (such as during productive phasic states), high-affinity alpha2 receptors are preferentially bound. In the prefrontal cortex, postsynaptic alpha2 activation inhibits adenylyl cyclase, closing hyperpolarization-activated cyclic nucleotide-gated (HCN) channels on dendritic spines. This molecular action strengthens functional synaptic connectivity within recurrent microcircuits, stabilizing goal representations and sharpening cognitive focus.  
* **alpha1-Adrenergic Receptors (alpha1-AR):** Lower-affinity, G_q-coupled receptors that stimulate phospholipase C (PLC), increasing intracellular calcium levels. As noradrenergic firing rises into elevated tonic bands (e.g., during acute environmental threat or high uncertainty), alpha1 receptors become occupied. Their activation disrupts prefrontal recurrent connectivity, effectively dismantling top-down cognitive control structures.  
* **beta-Adrenergic Receptors (beta1 and beta2):** Low-affinity, G_s-coupled receptors that stimulate cAMP production. Activated under high-concentration noradrenergic surges, beta-AR signaling strongly facilitates long-term synaptic plasticity and emotional memory consolidation in structures such as the amygdala and hippocampus, while simultaneously promoting sensory hypervigilance and a narrowing of attentional focus onto low-level physical threat features.1

The massive metabolic demands imposed by sudden noradrenergic network resets require immediate coordination with local energy delivery systems.1 Locus coeruleus varicosities densely overlay the cortical neurovascular unit (NVU), positioning noradrenergic terminals in direct proximity to astrocytes, capillary pericytes, and endothelial surfaces.2 When an animal encounters a high-urgency or volatile environmental shift, noradrenergic bursts act directly on astrocytic beta2-adrenergic receptors.1 This activation triggers a rapid intracellular signaling cascade mediated by adenylyl cyclase and the second messenger cAMP.1

This noradrenergic cascade stimulates rapid glycogenolysis within astrocytes—the sole cell type in the brain parenchymal structure that buffers glucose in the form of glycogen結構.1 This process breaks down glycogen into glucosyl subunits, entering a glycolytic chain that selectively generates lactate (L-lactate).1 This astrocytic lactate is rapidly exported into the extracellular matrix via monocarboxylate transporters (MCTs) and imported into adjacent active neurons to serve as an immediate, high-efficiency oxidative fuel source (the astrocyte-to-neuron lactate shuttle).1 Noradrenergic activation thus links behavioral urgency directly to localized metabolic mobilization, ensuring that the heavy bioenergetic cost of rapid policy revaluation and network stabilization is fully funded in real time.1

Concurrently, the LC-NE system regulates structural hyperemia at the neurovascular unit.2 Depending on the specific frequency of locus coeruleus activation and target vascular receptor layout, norepinephrine can evoke localized vasoconstriction or vasodilation.33 Low-frequency electrical stimulation (15 Hz) or pulsed bursts promote vasoconstrictive tension via vascular smooth muscle and pericytes, adjusting regional cerebral blood flow (rCBF) to steer perfusion precisely toward high-priority computational networks while preserving global systemic energy limits.33

## **6. Acetylcholine: Sensory Precision and Learning Gate**

The central cholinergic system, directed by distinct projection networks within the basal forebrain—including the nucleus basalis of Meynert (NBM), the medial septum, and the vertical/horizontal diagonal bands of Broca—acts as a primary modulator of sensory precision weighting and attentional gain within the predictive coding engine.16 Rather than functioning as a generic "memory chemical," acetylcholine (ACh) formalizes the statistical estimation of environmental cue reliability.19 It adjusts the balance between bottom-up sensory evidence and top-down prior beliefs, gating cortical plasticity to match the informational value of incoming prediction errors.16

Within the active inference framework formulated in Report C, the brain minimizes variational free energy by updating its internal generative models through precision-weighted prediction errors.19 The computational belief-updating equations define this relationship:

dmu/dt = sum_i (Pi_i * epsilon_i)

Here, epsilon_i represents the raw sensory prediction error vector ascending from lower hierarchical tiers, mu represents the internal hidden state belief, and Pi_i represents the precision weighting matrix—the inverse variance of the specific error channel.19

Acetylcholine serves as the primary biological mechanism for adjusting this precision weighting parameter (Pi_i).19 When acetylcholine release rises within sensory cortices, it selectively increases the gain of ascending prediction errors from attended channels while dampening the influence of top-down intracortical priors.19 Acetylcholine release provides a statistical report that the current environment contains a high level of expected uncertainty—meaning the baseline environment is noisy, but the incoming sensory cues are highly reliable and worth listening to for updating internal models.32 This chemical adjustment increases sensory accuracy and accelerates model updating, allowing the organism to track subtle environmental alterations rapidly.19

The functional architecture of cholinergic modulation operates through a precise division of labor between fast ionotropic and slow metabotropic receptor pathways 16:

* **Nicotinic Acetylcholine Receptors (nAChRs):** Pentameric ligand-gated ion channels (primarily alpha7 homomers and alpha4beta2 heteromers in cortical layers) that function as non-selective cation channels permeable to Na+, K+, and Ca2+.35 They mediate rapid, highly localized cholinergic gain adjustments. Activation of pre- and postsynaptic nAChRs on thalamocortical terminals directly boosts the release of glutamate, amplifying the initial entry of bottom-up sensory prediction errors into Layer IV input structures.16  
* **Muscarinic Acetylcholine Receptors (mAChRs):** Metabotropic GPCRs divided into five distinct structural subtypes (M1-M5).35 Subtypes M1, M3, and M5 couple to G_q proteins, while M2 and M4 couple to G_i proteins.37 Acetylcholine acts via M1 and M3 receptors on pyramidal neurons to suppress slow potassium currents (I_M and I_AHP), increasing intrinsic neuronal excitability and preventing spike-frequency adaptation. Simultaneously, acetylcholine activates presynaptic M2 or M4 autoreceptors on intrinsic horizontal intracortical connections, suppressing recurrent glutamate release.37 This combined action facilitates clear incoming sensory information while dampening internal lateral connections, preventing the premature imposition of uncalibrated top-down prior expectations.35

Acetylcholine and norepinephrine work as complementary statistical trackers.18 While acetylcholine tracks expected uncertainty (calibrating the variance of known noisy channels), norepinephrine tracks unexpected uncertainty and state volatility (catastrophic structural shifts in the environment).31 During optimal tracking states, basal forebrain cholinergic volume transmission maintains a steady sensory precision grid.16 When an unexpected environmental transition occurs, a rapid locus coeruleus noradrenergic surge interacts with basal forebrain circuits, triggering an immediate shift in cholinergic discharge to gate wide-scale cortical plasticity and model re-indexing.16 Insufficient cholinergic gain prevents model updates, causing stuck priors and behavioral perseveration; excessive cholinergic gain induces hyper-precision, rendering every environmental fluctuation a meaningful error, which triggers sensory overload and breaks down global policy coherence.19

## **7. Serotonin: Patience, Inhibition, Temporal Horizon, and Aversive Regulation**

The central serotonergic system, originating within the dorsal and median raphe nuclei (DRN/MRN) of the brainstem, acts as a master chemical tuning parameter for behavioral restraint, temporal discounting, aversive processing, and environmental adaptability.18 Popular reductionist models characterizing serotonin as a simple "happiness chemical," or framing mood disorders as a direct consequence of a static "chemical imbalance," are unsupported by modern neurobiology.40 Serotonin does not generate passive euphoria; rather, it coordinates the organism's behavioral and computational response to adversity, modulating both the capacity to endure sustained environmental stress and the cognitive flexibility required to structurally adapt to critical contexts.39

The functional architecture of the serotonergic system can be operationalized through the Carhart-Harris and Nutt bipartite model.39 This framework demonstrates that serotonin enhances two distinct, opposing adaptive responses to environmental adversity, mediated through its two most prevalent and evolutionary distinct central receptor branches 39:

* **The 5-HT_1A Receptor Pathway (Passive Coping and Stress Moderation):** The 5-HT_1A receptor is a high-affinity, G_i-protein-coupled receptor heavily expressed on postsynaptic structures within limbic architecture (e.g., hippocampus, septum, amygdala) and as an inhibitory somatodendritic autoreceptor within the raphe nuclei.39 Activation of postsynaptic 5-HT_1A receptors triggers G_beta-gamma-mediated activation of G-protein-coupled inwardly rectifying potassium (GIRK) channels, inducing hyperpolarization and suppressing neuronal firing.43 This pathway serves as the brain's default stress-buffering mechanism, mediating passive coping—the capacity to tolerate, endure, and preserve behavioral continuity in the face of sustained, unavoidable psychological or physical pain without developing learned helplessness or catastrophic allostatic collapse.39 This pathway is progressively reinforced by conventional selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs).39  
* **The 5-HT_2A Receptor Pathway (Active Coping and Cognitive Plasticity):** The 5-HT_2A receptor is a lower-affinity, G_q-protein-coupled receptor densely concentrated on the apical dendrites of Layer V pyramidal neurons within high-level cortical association structures.39 Because of its low affinity, it remains minimally occupied under normal conditions but becomes engaged when extreme environmental adversity or critical environmental shifts trigger mass serotonergic surges from the raphe.39 Activation of the 5-HT_2A pathway stimulates phospholipase C, increasing intracellular calcium and triggering downstream cascades including brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) transcription and mammalian target of rapamycin (mTOR) activation.39 This pathway mediates active coping, opening a transient window of profound psychological, structural, and affective plasticity.39 It destabilizes rigid, entrenched prior assumptions and habitual behavioral policies, providing the cognitive flexibility required to fundamentally restructure internal models and adapt to changing survival conditions.39 This pathway serves as the direct primary target for classic psychedelic agonists.39

Beyond stress coping, serotonin regulates behavioral choice along the axis of time.38 Electrophysiological recordings from the dorsal raphe nucleus demonstrate that serotonergic neurons elevate their tonic firing rate while an organism actively waits for a delayed reward, maintaining this restraint as long as the expectation of return remains statistically viable.38 Serotonin functions as a biological modifier of the temporal discounting parameter (gamma), extending the organism's behavioral horizon.38 High serotonergic tone suppresses the impulsive valuation of immediate, low-value rewards, enabling the execution of high-patience policies targeted at higher-value, long-delay goals.38

Conversely, forebrain serotonin depletion accelerates temporal discounting, driving impulsive action (the failure to suppress prepotent motor outputs) and impulsive choice (the selection of immediate gains at the expense of long-term utility).38 Concurrently, serotonin tracks aversive prediction errors, providing a computational counterweight to dopamine by signaling the estimated probability of environmental punishment or negative utility.18 Through its rich receptor diversity—including the ionotropic 5-HT_3 gated cation channel and various G_i-coupled structures—serotonin updates aversive expectations, allowing the organism to adjust behavioral velocity and avoid costly ecological traps.18

## **8. Opioids and Endocannabinoids: Relief, Comfort, Pain, and Recovery**

The endogenous opioid and endocannabinoid systems act as homeostatic dampers, hedonic evaluators, and metabolic recovery managers within the adaptive regulation engine.20 Moving beyond the simplistic characterization of these systems as mere mechanical switches for analgesia or the generation of a transient "runner's high," these signaling arrays serve as core regulators of policy consummation, sensory safety registration, and the metabolic transition from active allostatic mobilization to protective systemic recovery.8

Neurobiological formulations establish a structural distinction between reward *wanting* (the motivational incentive salience that energizes foraging and policy execution) and reward *liking* (the actual hedonic pleasure derived from consumption).28 While dopamine directs the computational machinery of wanting, the endogenous opioid system gates the mechanics of liking.28 Anatomical mapping identifies specific hedonic hotspots—including the rostrodorsal shell of the nucleus accumbens, the caudal ventral pallidum (VP), and the brainstem parabrachial nucleus—where local enkephalin and beta-endorphin transients act on mu-opioid receptors (MORs) to directly amplify sensory pleasure.28 Microinjections of MOR agonists into these localized hotspots increase positive hedonic behavioral readouts to sweet tastes and social safety cues, whereas MOR antagonists eliminate hunger-induced or cue-driven increases in baseline consumption comfort.28

The endogenous opioid array achieves context-sensitive control via three principal GPCR families that drive sharply divergent behavioral profiles 20:

* **Mu-Opioid Receptors (MOR):** Coupled to G_i/o proteins, MOR activation inhibits presynaptic voltage-gated calcium channels and opens postsynaptic potassium channels, suppressing neural firing. In the midbrain, MOR signaling on GABAergic interneurons in the VTA disinhibits dopaminergic projection lines, inducing a coordinated wave of wanting and liking that reinforces successful policies. MOR signaling downregulates nociceptive sensory processing, indicating systemic safety and providing a profound relief policy.28  
* **Kappa-Opioid Receptors (KOR):** Activated primarily by dynorphins synthesized in response to chronic stress, intense physical trauma, or severe allostatic load.20 KOR activation directly suppresses dopamine release in the nucleus accumbens, driving dysphoria, psychomotor sedation, and conditioned place aversion.47 This pathway acts as a biological circuit breaker, halting high-energy extraction policies when the environmental or somatic cost structures become unsustainably destructive.49  
* **Delta-Opioid Receptors (DOR):** Co-localized with MORs and forming functional heterodimers, DOR signaling enhances structural stability, modulates baseline anxiety, and coordinates emotional regulation, facilitating long-term affective relief and policy flexibility.47

The endocannabinoid system—driven by the synthesis of lipid-derived retrograde messengers such as anandamide (AEA) and 2-arachidonoylglycerol (2-AG)—acts as a master synaptic regulator that stabilizes homeostatic baseline parameters following stress or physical expenditure. Operating via presynaptic cannabinoid type 1 (CB1) receptors in the central nervous system, endocannabinoids suppress the hyper-excitability of both glutamate and GABA lines by inhibiting voltage-gated calcium influx. During stress recovery, endocannabinoids downregulate the acute activation of the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, restoring baseline sensitivity. Crucially, endocannabinoid transients in the basolateral amygdala gate fear extinction learning. By selectively dampening the persistent precision of threat-predictive aversive memories, endocannabinoids allow the brain to register that a previously hazardous environment has transitioned back to a state of safety. This molecular dampening prevents the organism from locking into permanent hypervigilance policies, freeing energetic resources for metabolic recovery, systemic tissue repair, and behavioral flexibility.

Maladaptive lock-in occurs when these systems are artificially over-stimulated or structurally exhausted. If an organism adopts a permanent exogenous relief policy, endogenous synthesis collapses, trapping the predictive engine in a state where threats appear hyper-precise and aversive costs feel unmanageable, driving compulsive checking or absolute behavioral withdrawal.

## **9. Oxytocin and Vasopressin: Social Salience and Defensive Belonging**

The hypothalamic neuropeptides oxytocin (OXT) and arginine vasopressin (AVP), synthesized within the paraventricular (PVN) and supraoptic (SON) nuclei and released both systemically via the posterior pituitary and centrally through diffuse volume transmission, serve as the primary chemical architects of social cognitive ecology.9 Popular characterizations labeling oxytocin as a "love hormone" or a universal "cuddle chemical" are scientifically invalid. Oxytocin does not enforce unconditioned prosociality or global altruism.28 Instead, it functions as a highly context-sensitive tuner of social salience, altering the precision weighting of interpersonal information based on prior ecological safety or threat distributions.9

The Social Salience Hypothesis demonstrates that oxytocin increases the gain on sensory and predictive channels that process social cues (e.g., facial morphology, vocal prosody, group micro-expressions), enhancing the extraction of interpersonal meaning.28 Within an established environment of structural safety and familiarity, this salience amplification suppresses amygdalar hyper-reactivity, downregulates social anxiety, and enhances the calculation of collective trust, thereby reinforcing affiliation, maternal care, and pair-bonding policies.28

However, when an organism operates under conditions of perceived environmental hazard, structural scarcity, or social competition, oxytocin’s salience-boosting effects drive radically non-prosocial outcomes. Under threat priors, oxytocin amplifies in-group favoritism and defensive cohesion while concurrently intensifying out-group xenophobia, envy, mistrust, and preemptive aggression. Oxytocin does not manufacture love; it updates the precision of the social landscape, rendering interpersonal information highly meaningful for the ongoing configuration of survival policies.28

Arginine vasopressin (AVP) acts as a close structural and evolutionary partner to oxytocin but shifts the behavioral tuning matrix toward territoriality, social status defense, threat vigilance, and active physical mobilization.44 Projecting through distinct receptor distributions, AVP increases sympathetic autonomic tone and heightens responsiveness to environmental challenges. In social contexts, vasopressin modulates the enforcement of dominance hierarchies, male-typical territorial defense, and paternal protectiveness. High vasopressin tone biases the predictive engine to interpret ambiguous environmental shifts as active boundary violations or status threats, prioritizing high-vigor defense or preemptive structural stabilization over relaxed exploitation.

The computational necessity of these social tuning systems is formalized by Social Baseline Theory (SBT).9 Within an evolutionary active inference framework, the human brain treats close proximity to reliable, predictable social partners as its primary ecological default state—the baseline configuration.9 Proximity to a trusted social scaffolding array is registered by the central nervous system directly as a formal bioenergetic asset, completely interchangeable with metabolic resources like systemic glucose or localized glycogen reserves.9 The brain operationalizes this social baseline through two explicit ecological mechanisms: *risk distribution* ("safety in numbers"), which represents the individual outsourcing of absolute environmental threat vigilance to the collective group network, thereby downregulating individual amygdalar and noradrenergic threat monitoring systems; and *load sharing*, which involves the formal cognitive and physical distribution of regulatory effort across multiple distinct agents who share identical optimization goals.9

This bioenergetic outsourcing alters fundamental cognitive and sensory perception.9 Psychophysical experiments demonstrate that human subjects systematically overestimate the steepness of a literal physical hill (e.g., viewing a 25-degree incline as a 45-degree slope) when standing alone, particularly if they are physically fatigued, hypoglycemic, or in a low mood state—a perceptual adjustment designed to regulate motor motivation and prevent metabolic exhaustion.10 Crucially, when a subject stands next to a trusted friend, or simply visualizes a supportive social partner, this overestimation is attenuated: the hill is perceived as significantly less steep.10

To the predictive engine, social scaffolding literally flattens the energy cost profile of the environment.9 When social baseline expectations are violated via isolation or relationship loss, the brain re-indexes its resource boundaries, canceling its bioenergetic outsourcing policies, causing threats to appear closer, actions to feel heavier, and driving an immediate compensatory increase in sugar consumption to manually offset the loss of social energy scaffolding.9

## **10. Cortisol and HPA-Axis Signaling**

The hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis operates as the organism's primary macro-scalar system for allostatic resource mobilization and anticipated demand regulation.8 Popular cultural narratives that label cortisol as an inherently toxic "stress hormone" ignore basic physiological logic. Cortisol is an essential, highly adaptive chemical tuning parameter.8 Its core function is to systematically restructure the organism's metabolic ledger, ensuring that circulating energy substrates are immediately accessible to fund high-priority, high-vigor survival policies in response to anticipated environmental challenges.8

Cortisol achieves nuanced allostatic control by operating through a bimodal receptor architecture characterized by vastly different binding affinities and regional expressions:

* **Mineralocorticoid Receptors (MR / Type I):** Exhibit an exceptionally high affinity for endogenous glucocorticoids, remaining highly occupied (around 90%) under baseline, non-stressed states. Distributed densely within limbic structures—particularly the hippocampus—MR signaling maintains baseline neuronal excitability, preserves circadian rhythm architecture, and enforces the default operational boundaries of memory indexing and cognitive processing.  
* **Glucocorticoid Receptors (GR / Type II):** Display a significantly lower affinity (around 10-fold less) for cortisol, remaining unoccupied during standard baseline hours. GRs are engaged only when circadian surges occur (the morning cortisol awakening response) or when acute environmental prediction errors trigger massive HPA axis activation. GR activation initiates wide-scale transcriptional modifications, downregulating non-urgent genetic expression while activating pathways that facilitate long-term policy adaptation.

Under acute allostatic challenge, the paraventricular nucleus releases corticotropin-releasing factor (CRF), driving anterior pituitary adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH) exocytosis, which provokes cortisol secretion from the adrenal cortex. Systemically, acute cortisol alters the glucose economy by stimulating hepatic gluconeogenesis, inhibiting peripheral glucose uptake in musculoskeletal tissue, and accelerating lipolysis. This shifts energy distribution, steering glucose directly toward the central nervous system to fuel the heavy control costs of prefrontal calculation and rapid motor coordination.5 Centrally, acute cortisol interacts with noradrenergic transients to alter the active inference engine.31 It increases the gain on threat-relevant sensory lines, enhances the consolidation of high-priority aversive prediction errors, and biases policy selection toward immediate defensive or escape protocols, while temporarily suspending high-cost long-term planning, reproductive behavior, and digestive policies.

While acute HPA mobilization is highly adaptive, chronic, non-resolving allostatic load triggers a catastrophic structural shift operationalized as Mitochondrial Allostatic Load (MALT).8 Under unceasing environmental or psychological stress, sustained hypercortisolemia induces a state of glucocorticoid receptor resistance and disrupts the intracellular quality-control systems of the cell.7 At the subcellular level, acute low-dose cortisol exposure is neuroprotective, driving glucocorticoid receptors to form complex arrays with anti-apoptotic proteins such as Bcl-2 and translocate into the mitochondria to optimize calcium holding capacity, enhance membrane potential, and boost adenosine triphosphate (ATP) synthesis.7 However, chronic high-dose glucocorticoid exposure depletes mitochondrial Bcl-2 and Bag-1 complexes within critical areas like the prefrontal cortex and hippocampus.7

This depletion leaves the organelle vulnerable to excitotoxic insult and triggers a critical tipping point where mitochondrial resilience fails: controlled ROS generation shifts to unmitigated oxidative damage; transient calcium fluxes become pathological overload, triggering mPTP opening; and mitochondrial dynamics shift from adaptive fusion to unmitigated, irreversible mitochondrial fragmentation.8 This structural fragmentation destroys the organelle's spare respiratory capacity, leading to bioenergetic insolvency.8 Damaged mitochondria release mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA) and related Damage-Associated Molecular Patterns (DAMPs) into the intracellular matrix, directly activating sterile neuroinflammatory cascades via microglial toll-like receptors (TLRs).8 This transition from adaptive allostatic mobilization to chronic biological debt degrades synaptic plasticity, accelerates tau phosphorylation, and drives structural atrophy within prefrontal-hippocampal networks, locking the behavioral engine into a permanent state of reward blunting and effort avoidance.8

## **11. Metabolic Hormones**

Peripheral metabolic hormones do not merely regulate mechanical adiposity or digestive tract motility; they function as core central nervous system tuning parameters that update the Expected Value of Control (EVC), rewrite the effort-discounting curve, and adjust the selection boundaries of foraging and appetitive policies.12 Operating via specialized receptors concentrated within hypothalamic hubs (e.g., arcuate nucleus) and mesolimbic motivation centers (e.g., ventral tegmental area), these signals translate the body's real-time energetic ledger into behavioral direction.12

Insulin (secreted postprandially by pancreatic beta-cells) and leptin (secreted by adipose tissue in direct proportion to lipid storage mass) function as long-term somatic wealth trackers.22 Centrally, they act as satiation signals that report high baseline energy reserves, systematically lowering the computational value of high-risk or high-effort foraging behaviors.12 Leptin binds to leptin receptors (LepRs) expressed directly on dopaminergic neurons within the VTA.12 Chemogenetic activation of VTA LepR-expressing neurons significantly reduces the willingness to work for rewards.12 In progressive ratio (PR) operant testing, leptin administration drives a severe reduction in active lever-press breakpoints, selectively discounting the value of high-effort behavioral investments without altering the standard consumption of food when it is freely available in the immediate environment.12 Leptin also attenuates sensitivity to reward-predictive cues, downregulating drug-seeking behavior and reducing conditioned place preference for substances of abuse during extinction phases.12 Central insulin signaling operates concurrently with leptin to modulate synaptic plasticity, enhance striatal dopamine transporter (DAT) activity, and suppress excessive dopamine transients, reducing the salience of environmental food cues. In states of chronic obesity, high-fat diet exposure induces central leptin and insulin resistance, blinding the brain to peripheral reserves.12 The active inference engine interprets this receptor silence as a state of imminent starvation, flattening the dopamine signal and locking the organism into high-urgency, short-horizon consumption policies.

Ghrelin—an orexigenic peptide synthesized by gastric endocrine cells—rises sharply during fasting states and drops immediately postprandially, functioning as a high-urgency metabolic prediction error that reports energy deficit.22 Ghrelin acts directly within the VTA by binding to the growth hormone secretagogue receptor type 1a (GHSR).12 This binding increases the firing rate of dopaminergic prediction lines to the nucleus accumbens, directly reshaping the effort-discounting matrix.12 Intrastriatal and intra-VTA ghrelin surges raise progressive ratio breakpoints for sucrose and external rewards.12 This effect is blocked by administering D1 or D2 receptor antagonists within the accumbens or naltrexone in the VTA, confirming that ghrelin recruits the classic mesolimbic incentive engine.12 Ghrelin-driven signaling triggers generalized arousal, wakefulness, and hyper-vigilant goal-directed locomotion, optimizing the behavioral economy to favor high-vigor foraging and environmental novelty-seeking under conditions of metabolic scarcity.12

Glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1), released from intestinal L-cells upon nutrient entry and synthesized centrally by preproglucagon (PPG) neurons in the nucleus tractus solitarius (NTS) of the hindbrain, acts as a primary postprandial satiation manager.12 GLP-1 receptors (GLP-1R) are distributed throughout the lateral septum, hypothalamus, and the mesolimbic reward system.12 Central or intra-VTA administration of GLP-1R agonists (e.g., exendin-4) suppresses operant responding for sucrose, dampens conditioned place preference for highly palatable choices, and significantly lowers meal size and frequency over extended cycles.12 Mechanistically, GLP-1 increases dopamine uptake and clearance within the striatum by driving elevated surface expression of the dopamine transporter (DAT).13 This accelerated clearance attenuates the baseline amplitude of reward-anticipation spikes, lowering food cravings and reducing functional neural hyperactivity within the insula, hypothalamus, and orbitofrontal cortex during exposure to highly rewarding cues.13 Beyond reward dampening, GLP-1R engagement activates neuroprotective intracellular pathways.13 It increases levels of anti-apoptotic proteins such as Bcl-2 while downregulating pro-apoptotic targets (bax, caspase-3), elevating intracellular cAMP to reduce central neuroinflammation, microglial activation, and cellular oxidative stress.13

## **12. Cytokines and Immune Signals**

The central registration of peripheral immune activation operates as a highly evolved motivational retuning program, completely challenging the unscientific reductionism that frames inflammation as a direct, mechanical cause of clinical depression.14 Within an active inference framework, cytokines (IL-1beta, IL-6, TNF-alpha, and interferons) function as real-time metabolic status indicators that inform the brain of a severe internal bioenergetic crisis: the massive, non-negotiable energetic investment required to fund an active immune response and sustain elevated body temperatures.15 Sickness behavior represents an organized, highly adaptive regulatory policy designed to optimize resource allocation during survival crises.15

When the innate immune system detects cellular damage or pathogen invasion, it releases systemic cytokines that communicate with the central nervous system through two primary pathways: a fast neuronal route via direct activation of peripheral vagal afferents projecting to the nucleus tractus solitarius; and a humoral fluid route, where circulating cytokines interact with brain endothelial cells within leaky circumventricular zones, driving a down-regulation of tight-junction proteins such as claudin-5.26 This structural breakdown renders the blood-brain barrier porous, permitting the entry of peripheral signaling elements and prompting endothelial cells to synthesize and release prostaglandin E2 (PGE2) into the brain parenchyma.26 Once central microglial activation is triggered, the brain executes the behavioral phenotype of sickness behavior: profound lethargy, anhedonia-like reward blunting, social withdrawal, hypersomnia, hyperalgesia, and anorexia.14 This is not a state of passive structural failure or accidental cognitive exhaustion; it is an active, highly orchestrated resource preservation policy.15 The energetic cost of running an immune defense program is exceptionally high.15 To fund this critical physiological defense, the active inference engine must aggressively terminate all non-essential external expenditures—such as physical foraging, territorial defense, mating rituals, and complex social interactions.15

To implement this external conservation policy, cytokines target the midbrain monoaminergic architecture.14 Central inflammation disrupts the synthesis, packaging, and release kinetics of dopamine within the basal ganglia and corticostriatal circuits.14 Elevated IL-6 and interferon-alpha (IFN-alpha) increase the expression and velocity of presynaptic dopamine transporters (DAT), rapidly clearing dopamine from the synaptic cleft and decreasing overall baseline turnover.14 Simultaneously, inflammation drives elevated intracellular glutamate accumulation within the striatum by downregulating astrocytic glutamate transporters, inducing localized excitotoxic stress and damaging dopaminergic prediction line integrity.27 This neuroinflammatory reduction in ventral striatal dopamine alters the Expected Value of Control, steepening the organism's effort-discounting curve.15

Computational behavioral modeling demonstrates that this immunometabolic shift alters the perceived capability parameter rather than a raw hedonic preference—it shifts the system from a state of preference-driven refusal ("won't") to absolute, structurally enforced capability insolvency ("can't").15 When inflammation transitions from an acute, self-limiting defense wave into a chronic, low-grade systemic state, this motivational flattening becomes structural.14 Persistent cytokine exposure locks the mesolimbic engine into a permanent state of effort avoidance and motivational blunting, mimicking the clinical architecture of treatment-resistant major depression.14

## **13. Other State Regulators**

To ensure complete homeostatic stability, the chemical tuning-board model integrates a distinct tier of hypothalamic and brainstem state regulators that govern behavioral boundaries, maintain arousal equilibrium, and enforce circadian gating parameters.21

Orexin (hypocretin-1 and -2) peptides, synthesized exclusively by a specialized population of glutamatergic neurons within the lateral hypothalamic area (LHA), function as master structural stabilizers of the brain's behavioral state architecture.21 Orexinergic neurons receive complex inputs regarding systemic metabolic status (stimulated by ghrelin, inhibited by leptin and high glucose), circadian timing, and limbic emotional states.21 They project to all major monoaminergic and cholinergic arousal centers, sending their densest noradrenergic activation lines directly to the locus coeruleus.21 Direct orexinergic stimulation depolarizes LC neurons, raising the global arousal threshold and preventing accidental, disorganized transitions between wakefulness, non-rapid eye movement (NREM), and rapid eye movement (REM) states.21 Genetic knockout of the prepro-orexin gene or structural deletion of orexin-2 receptors (Hcrt-R2) results in profound behavioral state instability, producing a classic narcoleptic phenotype characterized by sudden, unmediated sleep attacks and cataplexy during high-emotion wakefulness.21 Orexin ensures the structural continuity of selected behavioral policies, funding wakefulness maintenance during high-priority foraging or threat encounters.50

Histamine, synthesized by tuberomammillary nucleus (TMN) neurons within the posterior hypothalamus via histidine decarboxylase (HDC), fires tonically during wakeful states to maintain high-level qualitative cognitive wakefulness and slow-wave cortical suppression.55 Orexinergic and histaminergic neurons maintain reciprocal excitatory connections, cooperating to adjust the baseline cognitive gain of the cortex.55 While orexin directs the energetic and locomotive execution of behavioral states, histamine manages the qualitative attentional processing required for complex environment assessment.56

Melatonin, synthesized and released by the pineal gland under the direct synaptic control of the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) in response to light-dark cycles, acts as a global circadian gatekeeper. Melatonin does not function as a mechanical sedative or a direct neurochemical muscle relaxant; rather, it provides a systemic temporal signal that updates the prior probability distribution of sleep states. By binding to high-affinity MT1 and MT2 receptors throughout the brain, melatonin coordinates the temporal down-regulation of metabolic activity, preparing the active inference engine to transition away from high-cost external exploitation policies and toward internal tissue repair, metabolic consolidation, and synaptic pruning regimes.

Sex steroids (testosterone and estrogens) enter via passive vascular crossover, binding to androgen and estrogen receptors distributed across the amygdala, hypothalamus, and striatum to modify the calculated valuation of social dominance, territorial protectiveness, and reproductive allocation choices. These signals modify behavioral probabilities within existing socio-cultural constraints rather than dictating fixed behavioral outputs. Similarly, thyroid hormones (T3 and T4) cross via specialized iodothyronine transporters, targeting nuclear thyroid hormone receptors expressed across all central cellular structures to establish the fundamental basal metabolic rate of the nervous system, modulating global transcript execution and neural excitability parameters.31

## **14. Chemical Interaction Web: The Mixing Board**

The central nervous system does not operate through isolated, single-molecule pathways; it functions as a highly integrated, non-linear chemical mixing board. The dynamic combinations of these signaling systems combine to generate the organism’s current motivational weather—the net biochemical configuration that renders specific behavioral policies computationally attractive, urgent, effortful, aversive, safe, or physically impossible. For instance, the intersection of dopamine and cortisol during acute allostatic challenges accelerates dopaminergic baseline velocity, lowering the computed cost of musculoskeletal effort to fund high-vigor escape or extraction behaviors.5 However, if this interaction transitions into chronic hypercortisolemia, glucocorticoid receptor resistance develops, which downregulates dopamine synthesis and induces a severe flattening of the mesolimbic incentive landscape.7

This mixing board logic is further illustrated by the cross-talk between monoamines and peripheral metabolic or immune signals. Elevated circulating cytokines act directly on midbrain nodes to upregulate surface dopamine transporter velocity, stripping dopamine from the synaptic matrix.14 This biochemical shift interacts with baseline noradrenergic tone to flatten the Expected Value of Control, transforming an exploratory or exploitative policy layout into an unviable bioenergetic configuration, thereby locking the organism into a low-vigor conservation profile.15

Similarly, the balance between norepinephrine and acetylcholine determines the precise ratio between expected and unexpected uncertainty tracking: moderate acetylcholine release optimizes sensory precision weighting for stable cue-extraction 19, but a sudden noradrenergic surge completely overrides muscarinic lateral dampening, resetting cortical network configurations to force top-down generative model re-indexing.31 Neuropeptides alter these balances further; oxytocin combines with local mu-opioid receptor activation in striatal hedonic hotspots to amplify the positive hedonic tone and sensory precision of social signals, validating bioenergetic outsourcing under safety priors.9 If the social matrix transitions to a threat prior, vasopressin and low-affinity adrenergic signaling interact to dismantle prefrontal top-down control, shifting the behavioral topology from relaxed affiliation to hypervigilant status defense or territorial mobilization.31

## **15. Tonic, Phasic, Chronic, and Developmental Timescales**

To prevent the common operational error of time-collapse—mistaking a rapid channel adjustment for a long-term structural phenotype—the chemical tuning-board model maps regulatory events across an explicit chronological continuum. At the millisecond-to-second tier, dynamics are driven by fast ionotropic receptor channels and localized vesicular exocytosis. Nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (alpha7 and alpha4beta2) open non-selective cation pores within milliseconds to amplify bottom-up sensory entry channels 35, while phasic dopaminergic bursts from the VTA deliver temporally precise reward prediction errors (delta_n) that instantly shift the balance between direct and indirect striatal pathway activation.5 Noradrenergic bursts from the locus coeruleus trigger immediate cortical network resets to reorient attentional gain.31

Over timelines spanning minutes to hours, neuromodulatory volume transmission and metabotropic GPCR cascades alter regional network states. Extended operational timelines (days to weeks) drive changes in receptor density, enzyme velocity, and gene transcription profiles. At the macro-scalar scale of months, years, and lifespans, chronic chemical exposure alters the physical structure of the brain. Chronic, unresolving hypercortisolemia drives Mitochondrial Allostatic Load (MALT), triggering irreversible mitochondrial fragmentation, prefrontal-hippocampal dendritic atrophy, and systemic neurovascular degradation.7 Early-life attachment trauma or chronic developmental isolation programs the baseline distribution of oxytocin and vasopressin receptors, altering social salience calibration for adulthood.9 Finally, accumulated neurodegenerative stress drives phosphorylated tau aggregation first within the locus coeruleus, destroying noradrenergic projection density and permanently impairing functional hyperemia, the blood-brain barrier, and overall cognitive reserve.2

## **19. Mechanism / Model / Metaphor Audit**

To transition the canon to an expert analytical state, the explanatory models embedded within standard neurobiological discourse must be rigorously audited against active inference mechanics.

The popular "pleasure juice" metaphor frames dopamine as an internal reward substance released upon policy completion to generate subjective joy. This model fails to explain why dopaminergic neurons shift their firing pattern back in time to the earliest predictive cue, or why they fire below baseline when an expected reward is contractually withheld.18 The temporal difference reinforcement learning model resolves these contradictions by framing dopamine as an informational currency (delta_n) that updates value functions during policy navigation.18 It does not reward completion; it updates the statistical probability that a path is worth pursuing, dynamically altering action invigoration based on the long-run average rate of environmental returns (R).4

Similarly, the "happiness fluid" narrative treats serotonin as a baseline tonic stabilizer that prevents sadness, a view completely unequipped to explain why 5-HT_2A receptor agonists (like classic psychedelics) produce acute, profound, and often destabilizing reconfigurations of conscious and cognitive structures rather than static contentment.39 The Carhart-Harris bipartite model replaces this with a precise engineering framework: serotonin alternates between a default, high-affinity stress-moderation brake (5-HT_1A for passive coping) and a low-affinity, high-urgency system destabilizer (5-HT_2A for active coping) that opens a transient window of profound structural plasticity to rescue the active inference engine when it becomes trapped in unviable, high-stress loops.39

Finally, the "bad stress chemical" narrative treats cortisol as a biological error that damages health and should be universally suppressed, ignoring that without the morning cortisol awakening response, the organism cannot generate the blood pressure, glucose mobilization, or mental alertness required to initiate interaction with the environment. ## **23. Canon Handoff Summary**

### **Ten Core Preservation Principles**

1. **Chemical Control Parameters:** Reject single-molecule "mood chemical" folklores; treat chemical arrays as context-sensitive parameters adjusting precision, vigor, and learning rates.17  
2. **Tonic Dopamine as Time Cost:** Maintain the computational formulation where tonic striatal dopamine reports the long-run average reward rate (R), functioning as the opportunity cost of sloth.4  
3. **Biphasic Noradrenergic Gain:** Preserve the dual operational states of the locus coeruleus: task-locked phasic focus vs. high-volatility tonic network reset driving environmental exploration.29  
4. **ACh Sensory Precision Tuning:** Route acetylcholine as the formal manager of expected uncertainty, amplifying bottom-up prediction error precision while dampening horizontal priors.19  
5. **Bipartite Serotonergic Coping:** Map serotonin through its structural receptor bifurcation: 5-HT1A hyperpolarizing stress moderation vs. 5-HT2A structural cognitive plasticity.39  
6. **Liking vs. Wanting Disconnection:** Isolate mesolimbic dopaminergic wanting (incentive salience) from endogenous opioid mu-receptor hedonic hotspot liking validation.28  
7. **Social Scaffolding Asset Value:** Integrate Social Baseline Theory, treating proximity to trusted social resources directly as a formal bioenergetic asset that flattens environmental cost profiles.9  
8. **Mitochondrial Allostatic Load (MALT):** Track the tipping point where chronic hypercortisolemia transitions from adaptive resource mobilization to irreversible mitochondrial fragmentation.7  
9. **Metabolic Effort Valuation:** Route peripheral signals (GLP-1, leptin, ghrelin) directly into midbrain nodes to dynamically rewrite the computed Expected Value of Control.12  
10. **Immune-Motivational Retuning:** Institutionalize sickness behavior as an active resource preservation policy that downregulates striatal dopamine to fund systemic physiological defense.15

### **Ten Overclaim Patterns to Avoid**

1. **The Pleasure-Juice Trope:** Do not write that dopamine release directly manufactures sensory pleasure or hedonic satisfaction.28  
2. **The Dopamine Detox Illusion:** Avoid claiming that human receptor sensitivities can be mechanically reset through brief behavioral fasting.  
3. **The Serotonin-Deficit Myth:** Reject the narrative that clinical major depression represents a direct volumetric shortage of serotonin fluid in a static brain tank.40  
4. **The Love Hormone Generalization:** Never formulate oxytocin as a universal guarantee of unconditional empathy or non-exclusive prosocial trust.28  
5. **The Cortisol as Poison Fallacy:** Do not define cortisol as an inherently toxic element, omitting its essential role in circadian energy distribution and resource access.8  
6. **The Aggression Hormone Essentialism:** Avoid stating that testosterone drives unmediated physical violence, ignoring its modulation of context-dependent status defense.  
7. **The Willpower in a Syringe Metaphor:** Reject claims that GLP-1 agonists inject top-down cognitive willpower, omitting their acceleration of striatal dopamine clearance.13  
8. **The Adrenal Fatigue Diagnostic Folklore:** Never adopt the invalid non-clinical claim of exhausted cortisol synthesis pools, replacing it with central GR desensitization models.8  
9. **The Correlation to Phenotype Leap:** Avoid transforming population-level neurochemical association metrics into deterministic individual behavioral diagnoses.  
10. **The Model-to-Prescription Conversion:** Do not convert descriptive systems-neurobiology maps into immediate clinical or nutritional supplement intervention prescriptions.
