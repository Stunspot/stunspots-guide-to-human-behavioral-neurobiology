# B. Operational Epistemology, Evidence Tiers, and Causal Humility

## **Executive Orientation**

This report establishes the foundational operational epistemology, evidence evaluation architecture, and inferential discipline governing the knowledge-base canon for the Cognitive Ecology of Adaptive Regulation. Operating as the structural bridge between the multiscale ontology defined in prior foundations and the applied investigations in subsequent updates, this document establishes the validation rules for all subsequent claims.

The distributed, scale-free nature of regulatory ecologies introduces severe challenges for empirical verification. Because adaptive systems maintain structural coherence across nested scales using active inference and allostatic regulation, investigators are consistently vulnerable to cross-level interpretive errors. A claim is not scientifically validated merely because it utilizes the language of neuroscience, computational psychiatry, or graphical modeling. Rather, a proposition achieves legitimacy only when its warrants, boundary conditions, levels of analysis, and underlying uncertainties are explicitly mapped and quantified.

The governing thesis of this epistemology states that in complex adaptive human systems, explanation requires matching the claim type to the evidence type, causal level, timescale, population, ecological context, and scope of inference. This report operationalizes this thesis. It provides the canon with an anti-bullshit immune architecture, ensuring that elegant systems stories do not degrade into false certainty. By specifying the exact evidentiary voltage required for every claim, this framework gives future reports the tools to maintain analytical power without falling into reductionist overreach.

## **Inherited Canon from Report A**

The structural substrate of this canon models cognition as a distributed, scale-free regulatory ecology that operates under strict metabolic and thermodynamic constraints. This architecture treats adaptive systems as nested entities that maintain structural and functional coherence across biological, psychological, and environmental scales. This self-organizing dynamics is formally governed by active inference, predictive processing, and allostatic regulation, wherein systems minimize variational free energy to preserve internal states within viable bounds.

Statistical boundaries between internal, sensory, active, and external states are formally modeled as Markov blankets. These boundaries decouple internal systemic dynamics from environmental fluctuations while providing a structured interface for bidirectional information exchange. At macroscopic scales, social, technological, and cultural structures extend these regulatory mechanics through specialized interfaces, environmental affordances, behavioral cues, and institutionalized regimes of expectation.

While this inherited ontology provides a powerful integrated vocabulary, it introduces significant risks of interpretive error when structural dynamics at one scale are treated as sovereign or directly translated across boundaries without identifying the necessary mediating mechanisms. This report provides the epistemic calibration required to govern these concepts. It establishes the rules for determining when terms like Markov blankets or active inference function as literal physical descriptions, formal mathematical models, or conceptual heuristics.

## **Operational Epistemology**

Knowledge acquisition within this canon requires a strict taxonomy of evidence types to prevent the inferential errors common to contemporary pop-neuroscience and behavioral reductionism. The epistemic integrity of a distributed regulatory framework depends on distinguishing between the specific operations that generate data and the theoretical assertions derived from them.

### **Taxonomical Distinctions across Evidentially Valid Categories**

An observed fact constitutes a directly verified empirical occurrence, bounded by the precision parameters of the recording instrument. A measurement proxy is an operationalized, observable variable utilized to infer the status or dynamics of an unobservable latent construct. A statistical association establishes a mathematical relationship or covariance pattern between variables within specified confidence intervals, remaining inherently agnostic regarding directional or causal influence.1

Mechanistic evidence requires the isolation and empirical verification of the physical pathways, components, and operations through which a system produces a specific phenomenon.2 Causal inference represents the formal assignment of directional influence from an exposure to an outcome, validated through structural design elements or mathematical interventions that control for confounding variables.1 A computational model is a formal mathematical or programmatic simulation designed to evaluate the logical consistency, generative capacity, and theoretical boundary limits of a system, without serving as direct proof of physical realization.5

Clinical findings denote observations, diagnostics, or therapeutic outcomes derived directly from individuals experiencing functional impairment within structured medical or psychological treatment environments.7 Population-level patterns describe statistical distributions and epidemiological distributions across large groups, which are mathematically non-reducible to individual trajectories due to variance distribution.4 Ecological observations capture real-world behaviors and regulatory dynamics within naturalistic, unmanipulated environmental settings.9

Phenomenological or qualitative evidence captures the structural features of lived experience, subjective meaning-making, and first-person perspectives through systematized observational methods.10 A theoretical synthesis integrates diverse empirical data streams into a cohesive conceptual framework, identifying systemic regularities across distinct domains. A speculative bridge posits plausible but unverified connections across disparate operational scales, serving a heuristic discovery function to guide subsequent empirical validation.10 An explanatory metaphor leverages known physical or systemic processes to clarify complex organizational principles, providing conceptual accessibility without claiming structural identity. An intervention-ready claim denotes an operational proposition that specifies a precise target, leverage mechanism, clear boundary conditions, and predictable outcome vector sufficient to justify targeted strategic deployment.

### **Systemic Failures of Sovereign Epistemologies**

The inflation of any single epistemic category into a sovereign explanation undermines the analytical capacity of cognitive ecology. Conceptualizing human behavior solely through population-level epidemiological patterns results in ecological fallacies, projecting aggregate distributions onto highly specific, non-linear individual phenotypes.4 Conversely, relying exclusively on mechanistic neuroscience strips behavior of its indispensable socio-technological scaffolding, treating systemic cultural and institutional dynamics as mere epiphenomena of localized neurochemistry.2

Platform critiques and behavioral design methodologies routinely lapse into explanatory metaphor inflation, using terms like dopamine loops or brain rewiring to describe behavioral patterns without validating the underlying endocrinological or network architectures.8 This flattening of complex multi-scale systems into linear, mono-causal explanations creates severe clinical, design, and computational errors. By ignoring the statistical boundaries and precision-weighting operations that govern multi-scale regulation, such reductionism misidentifies transient environmental adaptations as permanent internal biophysical pathologies.

### **Operational Logic of Tier Compliance**

The classification of an empirical study within a high tier does not automatically grant validity to its broader theoretical claims. A systematic meta-analysis utilizing deeply flawed, poorly operationalized primary studies remains constrained by those foundational limitations.9 Crucially, an evidence stream is valid *only* for the specific level of analysis it directly measures. Mechanistic neuroscientific data derived from animal models can demonstrate structural biochemical pathways with high confidence, yet it possesses low confidence for explaining the complex semantics of human cultural institutions.

Epistemic strength within this canon requires matching research methodologies with the specific claims being evaluated. Moving from lower-tier observation to higher-tier intervention tracking requires systematic triangulation, ensuring that aggregate mathematical models are validated by explicit physical pathways and naturalistic behavioral tracking.

## **Causal Humility**

Causal humility represents a disciplined methodology designed to maximize the practical utility of behavioral models by establishing strict boundary conditions. Rather than weakening future investigations with vague qualifications, this practice systematically protects the canon against reductionism.

### **The Core Doctrine of Humility**

1. **Prohibition of Unmediated Level-Jumping:** No claim may transition across non-adjacent scales of analysis (e.g., from intracellular molecular signaling directly to political alignment) without explicitly mapping and empirically validating the mediating physical mechanisms and interfaces at every intermediate level.  
2. **Exclusion of Mechanism Claims from Correlation Alone:** Statistical covariance profiles between systemic inputs and behavioral outputs shall never be used to claim the discovery of a distinct physical pathway or specific biological mechanism.1  
3. **Prohibition of Individual Diagnosis from Population Patterns:** Aggregate statistical risk gradients and epidemiological probabilities derived from large demographic groups must not be used to diagnose or predict the specific functional trajectory of any single individual.4  
4. **Exclusion of Psychological Motivation from Behavioral Metrics:** Gross behavioral trace data, interaction frequencies, and platform utilization metrics measure action selection patterns; they do not provide direct proof of internal psychological motivations or distinct emotional states.  
5. **Requirement of Interface Routing for Social Explanations:** Macro-level structural and social explanations must specify the exact physical interfaces, somatic cues, and sensory inputs through which institutional constraints affect individual neurocomputational regulation.  
6. **Requirement of Developmental and Ecological Scope Checks:** Every biophysical and neurological explanation must be explicitly contextualized within the subject's developmental history and immediate cultural and physical environment, preventing the framing of context-dependent adaptations as universal traits.  
7. **Separation of Intervention Readiness from Explanatory Models:** Elegant conceptual models that clarify a behavioral phenomenon under controlled conditions do not justify real-world intervention design until they have been validated through targeted safety and efficacy testing.  
8. **Prohibition of Trait Inference from State Data:** Acute, context-dependent shifts in physiological markers or cognitive performance must never be interpreted as proof of stable, permanent individual psychological traits or fixed structural capacities.  
9. **Restriction of Pathology Claims:** A regulatory state or behavioral pattern shall not be labeled as a clinical pathology without documenting clear functional impairment, minimum duration thresholds, and context-inappropriate manifestation evaluated through multi-disciplinary standards.8  
10. **Prohibition of Moralization Disguised as Biology:** Scientific terminology and neurobiological mechanisms must never be used to pathologize or validate specific human choices, transforming socio-cultural preferences into objective biological hierarchies.  
11. **Eradication of "The Brain Does X" Rhetoric:** Phrases that anthropomorphize localized neuroanatomy must be replaced with language that describes specific multi-scale interactions between organisms, interfaces, and environments.2  
12. **Mandatory Documentation of Statistical Base Rates:** No diagnostic or predictive claims can be advanced regarding a specific psychological state without explicitly integrating the baseline prevalence of that state within the target population.12

### **Discipline in Cross-Level Pathways**

Moving responsibly among the distinct levels inherited from the foundational ontology requires identifying specific intermediate variables and boundaries. The canon strictly prohibits "teleportation claims"—inferential leaps that bypass essential regulatory machinery.

* **Molecule -> Cell -> Circuit -> Network -> Organismal State -> Behavior:** Claims tracing this cascade must identify the regional receptor densities, membrane potential alterations, effective connectivity variations, and macro-scale synchronization signatures that translate molecular adjustments into physical motor outputs.3 Teleportation claims that attribute macro social behavior directly to dopamine concentrations or cortisol shifts without mapping network-level signal modulation are structurally invalid.8  
* **Body State -> Affect -> Attention -> Action Selection:** This pathway requires tracking how interoceptive predictions modulate regional precision weights within agranular visceromotor cortices, shaping the global salience landscape. Investigators cannot infer stable attentional intent purely from peripheral autonomic arousal without establishing how internal sensory states alter prediction error hierarchies.  
* **Social Context -> Sensory/Active Interface -> Autonomic/Endocrine/Cognitive State:** Macro environmental contexts must be tracked through concrete sensory inputs (e.g., luminance arrays, acoustic frequencies) and active interfaces (e.g., digital touch surfaces). These inputs drive baseline HPA axis adjustments and autonomic tone.7 Asserting a direct biological link between socio-economic status and cognitive performance without isolating the specific material or physical cues that drive allostatic load is prohibited.7  
* **Institution -> Affordance/Deontic Cue/Resource Constraint -> Behavior:** Institutional rules shape systemic action selections by establishing physical resource limits and explicit social boundaries (regimes of expectation). To connect an institution to an individual action, reports must document how these structural rules modify the localized generative model, altering the subjective cost-benefit calculation of the agent.  
* **Platform Architecture -> Reinforcement Schedule/Attention Ecology -> Habit Formation:** Digital software environments modulate user behavior by implementing precise intermittent reinforcement schedules via sensory alerts.8 Connecting software layout to user habit formation requires measuring immediate interaction frequencies and tracking localized precision-weighting alterations over time, rather than relying on abstract metaphors of psychological manipulation.9  
* **Culture/Narrative -> Identity/Prior Beliefs/Social Expectation -> Regulatory Pattern:** Shared cultural narratives function as external generative structures that align individual predictive frameworks. Moving from a broad narrative to a biological regulatory pattern requires demonstrating how specific social expectations are internalized as baseline prior beliefs, modifying peripheral physiological regulation.  
* **Developmental Niche -> Baseline Priors/Allostatic Settings -> Adult Phenotype:** Early environmental structures establish long-term baseline allostatic parameters through epigenetic and circuit-level adaptations.7 Connecting a historical developmental niche to an adult behavioral phenotype requires mapping continuous prospective health indicators, preventing the use of retrospective self-reports to explain complex neurobiological traits.

## **Mechanism, Model, and Metaphor**

The conceptual architecture of this canon depends heavily on Karl Friston's Free Energy Principle (FEP), active inference, and allostasis. However, these concepts risk becoming overextended explanatory tools that mask a lack of empirical evidence with mathematical elegance.5 To prevent this, every analytical application must use a strict categorization framework.

### **Conceptual Taxonomy for Predictive Frameworks**

* **Literal Mechanism:** Applies exclusively to verified physical structures, biochemical pathways, and cellular operations. Examples include the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis cascade, systemic allostatic load accumulation, and localized neurotransmitter transmission.4 Language must specify physical components and measurable pathways.  
* **Formal Computational Model:** Applies to mathematical equations, graph-theoretic structures, and programmatic simulations utilized to study optimization logic. Examples include variational free energy equations, generative predictive processing algorithms, and machine learning pattern classifiers.5 These models assess conceptual validity but do not prove biological implementation.5  
* **Measurement Proxy:** Applies to observable, quantifiable variables used to track changes in unobservable latent states. Examples include functional blood-oxygen-level-dependent (BOLD) signaling used to track regional blood flow, or digital trace chains used to infer attentional focus.9  
* **Clinical Heuristic:** Applies to pragmatic, semi-standardized diagnostic frameworks used by clinicians to organize symptom profiles and guide treatment selection. Examples include allostatic overload indexes and behavioral addiction tracking matrices.8  
* **Ecological Metaphor:** Applies to conceptual frameworks that use systems terms to describe macro-level human behaviors without claiming direct biophysical equivalence. Examples include analyzing an urban environment or a digital platform as an active inference ecosystem. This usage organizes observation but carries zero causal authority.

### **The Markov Blanket Calibration**

The deployment of the Markov blanket construct requires strict discipline to prevent the conflation of mathematical abstractions with physical boundaries.5 Within graph theory and Bayesian networks, a Markov blanket is a formal statistical abstraction: a set of variables (sensory and active states) that conditionally decouples an internal set of variables from an external set.3

Problems arise when this statistical mapping tool is treated as a physical boundary in the world.5 In empirical neuroscience, identifying a Markov blanket across a single cell membrane represents a literal physical hypothesis supported by visible spatial boundaries.3 However, scaling this construct to explain macro-level phenomena—such as defining an individual's digital phone or a cultural institution as a literal Markov blanket—transforms the concept into an ecological metaphor.3

Such macro-level applications depend on subjective researcher assumptions regarding which variables matter and what timescales are under analysis.19 Consequently, these high-level applications must be explicitly framed as conceptual metaphors or formal models designed to organize information. They do not provide mathematical proof of a distinct biological boundary.5

### **The Mechanics of Reverse Inference Calibration**

The implementation of functional neuroimaging data within this canon must carefully navigate the logical problem of reverse inference.10 Forward inference moves from a controlled psychological state to an observed biophysical brain map, documenting that when a subject experiences a specific task, region X activates.10 Reverse inference attempts to run this logic backward, concluding that because region X is active, the subject must be experiencing that specific psychological state.10

This reasoning pattern represents a deductively invalid syllogism: the logical fallacy of affirming the consequent.10 Its validity depends entirely on the spatial and functional selectivity of the brain region in question.12 Because most macro-scale brain regions are highly non-selective—activating across diverse cognitive states—informal reverse inference has low predictive power.12

To utilize reverse inference responsibly, this canon distinguishes between two strategic implementations:

1. **Weak Reverse Inference (Heuristic Implementation):** Functions exclusively as a strategy for discovery.10 It prioritizes conceptual fertility over absolute certainty, using unusual brain activation patterns to generate novel hypotheses that are then subjected to empirical testing.10  
2. **Strong Reverse Inference (Justificatory Implementation):** Functions as a formal rule of acceptance.10 It requires large-scale functional databases (e.g., NeuroSynth) and machine learning multi-voxel pattern decoding to quantify the conditional probability of a psychological state given a specific activation pattern: P(COG|NEU).10 This approach requires explicitly accounting for regional selectivity limits and population base rates to validate its conclusions.12

## **Ecological Validity and Scope Conditions**

Transitioning behavioral insights from highly controlled laboratory environments to unconstrained everyday life requires a careful evaluation of ecological validity and scope conditions. Human regulatory dynamics are highly sensitive to contextual shifts, making direct generalizations prone to error.

### **The Breakdown of Laboratory Generalization**

Laboratory experiments purposefully strip away real-world complexity to isolate specific functional capacities, introducing significant artificial constraints. In a laboratory attention task, a participant operates within a quiet room, stripped of competing incentives, and driven by explicit instructions. In contrast, everyday life presents a complex environment where multiple interfaces, social pressures, and metabolic demands constantly compete for limited cognitive resources. Consequently, a performance metric captured within an artificial setting cannot be assumed to translate directly into real-world behavior.

Furthermore, much of the foundational data in cognitive neuroscience derives from WEIRD (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic) undergraduate populations tested within artificial laboratory settings. Projecting these specific data streams as universal models of human nature ignores how deeply cultural practices and physical environments shape underlying predictive architectures. Human systems adapt their predictive models to their localized socio-technological niches, meaning that behavioral findings must always be qualified by the cultural and economic contexts of the study sample.

### **Context Collapse and Generalization Errors**

Context collapse occurs when an empirical finding is stripped of its specific environmental parameters and mistakenly applied as a universal law across entirely different domains. For example, platform studies routinely rely on aggregate digital trace data, such as calculating total hours of daily screen utilization.7 This metric pools highly divergent behaviors—such as passive algorithmic video consumption, remote economic labor, and interactive social support—into a single artificial variable.7

Attempting to link this aggregate proxy to broad mental health outcomes creates severe generalization errors, masking how different interface designs modulate physiological arousal and attention.7 To avoid these errors, all subsequent analyses must explicitly document their environmental boundaries. They must specify whether data derive from clinical or non-clinical populations, acute lab exposures or chronic real-world environments, and passive or interactive behavioral interfaces.

## **Conflict Resolution Protocol**

When independent, credible scientific sources yield flatly contradictory empirical findings, this canon explicitly rejects the practice of averaging away differences or selecting data that fits a preferred narrative. Disagreement within the literature typically reflects underlying differences in measurement parameters or sample demographics.

### **Systematized Disagreement Dissection Protocol**

1. **Construct Definition Audit:** Deconstruct the exact operational definitions utilized by the opposing research teams. Determine whether they are measuring identical latent psychological variables or applying identical labels to distinct behavioral phenomena.9  
2. **Measurement Instrument Verification:** Inspect the technical specifications of the recording tools, task parameters, and self-report scaling architectures.7 Minor adjustments in digital trace capture or survey structure can fundamentally shift user response profiles.  
3. **Population and Subgroup Stratification:** Evaluate the demographic characteristics, geographic backgrounds, and baseline socio-economic profiles of the study samples to detect critical variations.4  
4. **Developmental Stage Verification:** Track the precise age brackets and developmental windows of the cohorts, filtering for age-specific neurodevelopmental trajectories and baseline allostatic characteristics.  
5. **Clinical vs. Non-Clinical Cohort Parsing:** Determine whether one data stream derives from individuals experiencing functional impairment within therapeutic settings, while the opposing data tracks healthy populations navigating normative stressors.7  
6. **Laboratory vs. Naturalistic Ecological Matrix:** Map whether the contradictory data reflect performance under highly controlled laboratory conditions or real-world naturalistic tracking.9  
7. **Human vs. Animal Model Evidence Separation:** Ensure that conserved molecular and micro-circuit insights from animal studies are not automatically equated with highly complex human psychological constructs.2  
8. **Acute Exposure vs. Chronic Accumulation Tracking:** Separate findings based on time horizons, distinguishing between short-term physiological responses to an acute stressor and long-term allostatic adaptations to chronic environments.1  
9. **Short-Term vs. Long-Term Outcome Modeling:** Evaluate whether measured outcomes capture temporary behavioral adjustments or trace permanent, long-term phenotypic adaptations.  
10. **Sample Size and Statistical Power Audit:** Check absolute participant counts, power thresholds, and effect size distributions to filter out underpowered statistical noise.  
11. **Replication History and Open Science Verification:** Run an audit across registered reports, open-science replication databases, and statistical funnel plots to identify systemic publication bias or structural field incentives.  
12. **Publication Bias and File-Drawer Assessment:** Estimate the potential impact of unpublicized negative results on the consistency of the broader literature stream.  
13. **Field Incentives and Disciplinary Boundaries:** Track the baseline theoretical commitments, academic dynamics, and publishing constraints of the respective scientific subfields.  
14. **Institutional and Commercial Funding Disclosures:** Document proprietary interests, platform ownership realities, or corporate funding streams that could introduce structural confirmation bias.  
15. **Theoretical Commitments and Paradigm Rigidities:** Identify hidden ideological assumptions or conceptual biases that color how empirical results are interpreted by the authors.  
16. **Cultural and Historical Context Analysis:** Situate the conflicting studies within their specific historical periods and dominant cultural frameworks to account for broader paradigm shifts.

## **Confidence Language Library**

To maintain rhetorical clarity and prevent inflation, all subsequent research reports within this canon must explicitly qualify their conclusions using a standardized confidence-language framework.

### **Standardized Confidence Tiers**

* **High Confidence:** Reserved for propositions supported by consistent, replicated evidence from multiple independent meta-analyses, well-powered prospective longitudinal cohorts, or rigorous double-blind randomized controlled trials, where construct operationalization is highly stable and ecological validity is explicitly mapped.4  
* **Moderate Confidence:** Applied to claims backed by substantial empirical data from well-designed cohort studies, natural experiments, or mechanistic laboratory models, where strong statistical associations exist but unresolved boundary conditions or minor measurement heterogeneity remain.4  
* **Low Confidence:** Assigned to assertions resting on limited empirical tracking, cross-sectional designs without robust confounding controls, single-center clinical case evaluations, or indirect behavioral trace indicators with known validity constraints.7  
* **Speculative:** Designated for useful conceptual hypotheses, innovative interdisciplinary syntheses, and preliminary exploratory models that serve a strategic discovery function but currently lack direct empirical verification or operational validation.10  
* **Not Established:** Explicitly used for highly visible, popularized claims that currently lack sufficient empirical data, rigorous operationalization, or replication history within the peer-reviewed literature.  
* **Contraindicated / Misleading:** Applied to popularized terminology, reductionist frames, or outdated scientific simplifications that actively distort empirical evidence and introduce clear analytical errors.

## **Annotated Bibliography**

### **Analysis of the Core Literature Assets**

* **The Markov Blanket Debate Realism vs. Instrumentalism [3, 5, 6, 19, 20]:** This literature stream traces the development of the Markov blanket from its standard origins in Judea Pearl's graphical models (epistemic tools mapping conditional independence) to its novel ontological role in Karl Friston's Free Energy Principle (realist boundaries demarcating physical agents). Bruineberg, Baltieri, and colleagues present a critical analysis, demonstrating that treating Friston blankets realistically requires heavy metaphysical assumptions that do not flow directly from the underlying mathematics. Spreading across scales requires careful choices regarding target variables and relevant timescales, meaning that macro-level applications function as useful conceptual metaphors or computational models rather than physical, objective truths.  
* **The Neuroimaging Reverse Inference Framework [10, 12, 13, 14, 17, 18, 21, 22, 23]:** Formulated by Russell Poldrack, this core methodology establishes that reverse inference—inferring specific latent cognitive processes from observed patterns of brain activation—presents an invalid logical architecture based on affirming the consequent. The analytical value of these inferences is strictly limited by the functional selectivity of the target brain region. While informal, anatomically coarse reverse inference has low predictive power, advanced multi-voxel pattern analysis (MVPA) decoding techniques paired with comprehensive large-scale databases (e.g., NeuroSynth) provide a structured, Bayesian approach to establish quantified probabilistic mappings. This framework splits reverse inference into a weak heuristic for strategy discovery and a strong, data-driven approach for model comparison.  
* **Epidemiological Causal Models and Bradford Hill Frameworks [1, 2, 4, 24]:** This literature details how medical statistics and epidemiology move from observed associations to verdicts of causation within complex human systems where controlled randomized designs are unfeasible. Austin Bradford Hill's classic framework outlines independent viewpoints—including temporality, biological gradient, consistency, and mechanism—to systematically evaluate evidence. Modern adaptations of this methodology demonstrate its extreme value for cognitive neuroscience, preventing individual studies from overclaiming causal authority while providing a structured path to build strong directional inferences by aggregating multiple independent, circumstantial evidence streams.  
* **Measurement Proxies and Digital Phenotyping Constraints [7, 8, 9, 11, 15]:** Evaluating the intersection of behavioral psychology, endocrinology, and digital media, these studies demonstrate the critical limits of indirect measurement proxies. Investigations tracking indicators like salivary cortisol, resting heart-rate variability, or aggregate device "screen time" reveal substantial heterogeneity in operational definitions. This variation highlights the risks of context collapse, where distinct behavioral activities are pooled into a single variable, masking how different interface layouts affect physiological stress profiles and sleep architecture over time.
